# The iPhone's Last Stand

**Date:** June 9, 2026  
**Source:** [Stratechery by Ben Thompson](https://stratechery.com/2026/the-iphones-last-stand/)

After Apple's bungled 2024 launch of Apple Intelligence and new Siri, however, vaporware is fair game...

The point of an agent is not to use the computer for you; it's to accomplish a specific task. Everything between the request and the result, at least in theory, should be invisible to the user. This is the concept of a thin client taken to the absolute extreme.

Apple Intelligence will know more about you than any other AI, because your phone knows more about you than any other device.

Consumers don't want to work, and don't really care about being productive. Agents help you do work and be more productive, and consumers don't want to work or care about being productive. What they do want to do is watch short-form video.

Apple is heavily incentivized to preserve the iPhone's importance, and by extension, to focus on use cases organized around human interaction.

### Microsoft's Project Solara – Thin Client Ecosystem for Enterprise

Announced at Build 2026. Vision: ecosystem of devices that are portals to cloud-based agents, not standalone computers. Agents compute on your behalf without constant human interaction. Positioned purely as an enterprise play.

### Apple's Siri AI – Improved but Still Interaction-Focused

WWDC 2026 demo showed Siri with context awareness and App Intents. Key limitation: Siri still operates within the interaction paradigm (user initiates, Siri responds). Apple's advantage: personal context from iPhone.

### Consumer Market Reality

Consumers don't care about productivity. "Good enough" is enough when consumers prioritize entertainment.

### Technical Details

Private Cloud Compute expanded to include Nvidia chips running in Google data centers. 20 billion parameter on-device mixture-of-experts (MoE) model.

### Strategic Comparison

Microsoft (Project Solara) focuses on enterprise, agents, cloud with enterprise subscription model. Apple (Siri AI) focuses on consumer, interaction, device with hardware + services model.

**Conclusion:** Apple's approach is correct for its market and incentives, but it misses the agentic paradigm that Microsoft is betting on for enterprise.
