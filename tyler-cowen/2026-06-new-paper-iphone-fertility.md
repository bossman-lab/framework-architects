# New Paper on the iPhone and Fertility

**Date:** June 2026  
**Source:** [Marginal Revolution](https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2026/06/new-paper-on-the-iphone-and-fertility.html)

The U.S. general fertility rate has fallen by 22% since 2007. Researchers assess the potential role of a different shock: the diffusion of the smartphone. The U.S. rollout of the iPhone provides a natural experiment — from June 2007 through February 2011, the device was sold only on AT&T.

Entropy-balanced Poisson and synthetic difference-in-differences event studies imply that access to the iPhone reduced births by 4.5–8.0% at ages 15–19 and 3.2–6.6% at ages 20–24. Overall, the diffusion of the iPhone explains 33–52% of the decline in the general fertility rate among women aged 15–44.

An interesting and difficult question is how much we actually want teen fertility rates to decline, and to what extent we should consider such declines a good thing.
