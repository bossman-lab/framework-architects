# Why Is America Less of a 24/7 Society?

**Date:** June 2026  
**Source:** [Marginal Revolution](https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2026/06/why-is-america-less-of-a-24-7-society.html)

It's deeply odd that America is a far less 24/7 hour society today than it was 10, 20, or even 30 years ago. In 1996 it was possible to buy groceries, clothing, a lawn mower, a snow blower, Lego sets, and bow hunting gear at 3 AM in a mid-sized Indiana town. That is long gone.

### Hypotheses

1. America is older. True, but hardly the main explanation.
2. Due to increasing leisure time, fewer people want to work weird and long hours.
3. It is *stores* that are in decline. 24/7 activity has moved into the warehouse, the fulfillment center, the server farm, the delivery network, and the home.
4. Shoplifting has become more common.
5. Online entertainment is much better, so why go out late at night?
6. More work from home means people are not returning from their jobs at late hours.

I would put most of my money on #3 and #5.
