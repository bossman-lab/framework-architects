# Safety and Nation-Building in Mexico

**Date:** June 2026  
**Source:** [Marginal Revolution](https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2026/06/safety-and-nation-building-in-mexico.html)

That is the topic of my latest Free Press column.

Mexico is still not a mature nation-state. Drug gangs may control as much as one-third of its territory. When Mexico won its independence from Spain in 1821, Central America joined only briefly and then split off. The state of Yucatán rebelled repeatedly. In the 1840s, the U.S. took away about half of its territory. The 1910 Mexican Revolution killed about 10 percent of the population.

The central government spends just short of 23 percent of GDP, relatively low for its development level. By contrast, Brazil spends over 32 percent. If the Brazilian government is too large, Mexico's is too weak and too small, most of all because Mexico cannot beat back its drug gangs by brute force.

Mexico as a topic will never become obsolete, not for the United States at least.
