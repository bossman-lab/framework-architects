# Again, the Research Paper Format Will Be Dying Out

**Date:** June 2026  
**Source:** [Marginal Revolution](https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2026/06/again-the-paper-format-will-be-dying-out.html)

From Xudong Han:

A paper co-authored by 37 authors from Stanford, CMU, Michigan, and elsewhere: *The Last Human-Written Paper*. The core argument is that the paper format we've been using for centuries might already be obsolete in the AI era.

### Two "Invisible Taxes"

1. **The narrative tax:** To tell a compelling story, we delete failed experiments, dead ends, and overturned hypotheses. What AI reads is a "walkthrough guide" but it misses the valuable "pitfall logs."

2. **The engineering tax:** Implementation details in papers are enough to convince reviewers, but not enough for an Agent to directly reproduce.

The authors propose ARA, transforming papers directly into "research packages" that Agents can read and execute. When AI also becomes a reader and executor of papers, should papers still look like they do today?

By the way, as a side point: due to writing detection abilities of AI models, anonymous referee reports are now a thing of the past.
