# A Simple Reason for Skepticism About the iPhone/Fertility Link

**Date:** June 2026  
**Source:** [Marginal Revolution](https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2026/06/a-simple-reason-for-skepticism-about-the-iphones-fertility-link.html)

In 2008, 1.9% is the share of the mobile-subscribing population with an iPhone. As a percent of all adults that is 1.6%. In 2009, it is 4.3% (3.6% of all adults). In 2010, 6.8% (5.5% of all adults).

Plus conception to birth takes nine months, noting that actual family planning may make this lag far longer. In 2008 fertility rates already were falling pretty sharply. The whole "maybe the iPhone messes up your dating processes" factor also requires some time to operate.

So when the authors talk about diffusion explaining 33–52% of the decline in the general fertility rate among American women 15–44, I still do not get how that is supposed to operate.

The explanations I am hearing seem to be parasitic on world intuitions from 2026, not the time period under consideration.
