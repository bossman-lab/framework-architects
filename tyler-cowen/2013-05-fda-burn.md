# Burned by the FDA

**Date:** May 2013  
**Source:** [Marginal Revolution](https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2013/05/fda-burn.html)

If you lived in Great Britain or Germany and your physician prescribed a pharmaceutical, would you ask them, "has this pharmaceutical been approved by the U.S. FDA?" Probably not. At FDAReview.org Dan Klein and I argue that international reciprocity is a no-brainer.

Eight sunscreen ingredient applications have been pending before the U.S. FDA for years — some for up to a decade — for products available in many overseas countries. The sunscreens available in the U.S. are not without risk and in some ways, the European standards are stricter than the US standards.

27 states have driver's license reciprocity with Germany. Why not pharmaceutical reciprocity?
