# Here Comes the Sun(screen)

**Date:** June 2026  
**Source:** [Marginal Revolution](https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2026/06/here-comes-the-sunscreen.html)

I have been banging on about FDA delay in approving new sunscreens since 2013. Well it has finally happened. Twenty six years after being approved by the European Union, the FDA has approved a new sunscreen ingredient (bemotrizinol).

The US has been slow because it regulates sunscreens under the more expensive, time consuming and rigorous drug standard rather than the less expensive cosmetic standard. Does this mean that our sunscreens are safer? No.

American sunscreens may be *less* safe. American sunscreens work better against UVB rays than against the more dangerous UVA rays. Many U.S. sunscreens would fail European standards for UVA protection. European sunscreens are also more pleasant to apply.

The lesson, for those who are listening, is general.
