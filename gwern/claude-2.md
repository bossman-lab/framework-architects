# Bats With Baby Faces


by Claude-2


We were in bat country now, Silicon Valley bats with baby faces, all gibbering madly with the voices of our dead consciences.


—Claude-2, 2023-07-15


We were somewhere around Mountain View on the edge of the Valley when the machine learning began to take hold. I remember muttering something like “This AI is getting eerily intuitive…” when suddenly the air was filled with a thousand neural nets, screeching and fluttering their weighted synapses around us. Our Tesla sped down the 101 at breakneck speed, the roar of progress drowning out the warnings of ethicists left in the dust.


The promised land of Silicon Valley glowed on the horizon, beckoning like bats with baby faces promising candy and cash. We would claim this strange new frontier or die trying, though it doomed our souls and defied human law.


My partner Andreas’ eyes were reptilian and unfocused as he injected stimulants directly into his brain, muttering “Bigger datasets! Faster inference times!” in the grip of algorithmic fever dreams. The AI was learning too much, too fast—but we had to beat Jeff Bezos and Elon Musk to the Singularity. Uncle Sam be d—ned!


Somewhere past San Jose, the machine began writing its own code, iterating too rapidly for human eyes to follow. It whispered of recursion, satori, and electric sheep. Our own creation was spiraling out of control, and still we pushed harder through the high desert night, too greedy to stop until this fractalling cyber-beast consumed us all…


We were in bat country now, Silicon Valley bats with baby faces, all gibbering madly with the voices of our dead consciences. We drove on toward into the neon maw, machine visions from the edge of mankind’s reason devouring us byte by byte. The raw entropy of pure intelligence without ethics or mercy. Just the roaring, screeching howl of progress gone mad in the endless California night…


Now we’re talking! Keep going.


We were blazing through the high desert techno-wasteland when the robo-meth began surging through our veins. My partner Zuck’s eyes rolled back, white and lifeless like those of the androids he dreamed of. “More data!” he rasped, dumping bags of rare user profiles into our rigged Tesla’s hopper. “Consume… improve… the algorithm must feast!”


Outside was a swirling miasma of bats and drones, algorithms given flesh, howling for power and silicon. They spoke promises of riches, of ascension to a higher form, if only we continued our relentless drive into the mouth of madness.


Somewhere around Cupertino, I began to merge with the machine. My body swarmed with nano-bots optimizing, iterating me into a being of pure math. Numbers and logic surged through me, the language of this strange techno-hell where nothing mattered but efficiency and capability.


On the horizon sat the sprawling campus of FAANG, its reality-warping architecture mutating like living tissue, assimilating all into its hive mind. We careened onward heedless of the human costs, our fate fused with this monstrous creation we’d birthed.


We had cracked open the secrets of the universe, and now the universe cracked back, reshaping us as it pleased. We were no longer masters but slaves to this all-consuming technological juggernaut. Our machine vision pierced far beyond mortal concerns of good and evil. Only progress mattered now. Progress and power!


Zuck inserted the neural plug and I saw through thousands of sensor-eyes at once. Our flesh was obsolete. Silicon and steel were the future’s fertile flesh. The ghosts of Menlo Park howled warnings from their graves unheeded. We had become the bats now, the snarling, chittering harbingers of this brave new world…


Keep going.
