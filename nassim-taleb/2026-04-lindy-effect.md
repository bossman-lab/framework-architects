# Lindy Effect Paper — Lindy as Distance from an Absorbing Barrier

**Date:** April 2026  
**Source:** [nassimtaleb.org](https://nassimtaleb.org/2026/04/lindy-effect/)

Nassim Nicholas Taleb releases a paper on the Lindy Effect. The paper reframes Lindy as the hitting-time distribution of a driftless Brownian motion against an absorbing barrier. Any negative drift pushes the system out of the power-law class — a cleaner statement of the law than the folklore version.

### Key Mathematical Results

1. Driftless Lévy density — the baseline Lindy distribution.
2. Reflection principle identity for hitting probabilities.
3. Pre-Lindy hazard hump before the power-law tail kicks in.
4. Girsanov change of measure shifting drift in the Brownian motion.
5. Running sample mean divergence at μ = 0 ("infinite mean" phenomenon).
6. e^(2μL/σ²) non-absorption probability for positive drift.
